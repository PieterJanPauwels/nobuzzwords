Ghent

Welcome to No Buzzwords Ghent. We made this webpage for SCWE as a small guerilla intervention to explain how we see Smart Cities as a concept. Feel free to read our short manifest on how we really view Ghent as a smart city: a city of people. Or just download our digital version of the buzzwords bingo below. Your choice.

Ghent: a "City of People", OUR city!

Ever walked around a city and wondered what makes it tick? Why is it so different from others? Why is Ghent so rebellious?

Every city has it’s own beat, vibe, feeling... We should not try to conform to one city manual or ideal but evolve in accordance to our own DNA, history, data and stakeholders. We have no ambition in Ghent to become a smart city, this buzzword has become meaningless. We want to keep evolving in function of the future we determine. Which future do we want to see for Ghent? And no we will never arrive and keep on adapting and changing.

Every location has specific challenges and is confronted with wicked problems. Choices, priorities and future ambitions are chosen within the communities. These are the basis of a common vision. "In Ghent, the focus lies on US in the city of the future. Smart cities cannot exist without their smart citizens", no boasting intended but if we use buzzwords best go all the way

Our model of a future city puts us back in the centre of our environment and the city, creating more efficient and optimal links between the available means, technology, communities, services and events in the urban environment. All these links also mean that we, as citizens of Ghent, no longer watch and wait for our governments but are empowered to take up our own responsibility for our environment.

In Ghent, we summarize this as inclusive, innovative and sustainable (buzzwordalert: we will have to find a more concrete word). We are uniting within the Ghent Municipality, companies, research institutions to optimize the opportunities and the challenges of the knowledge and information society.

We used to hear people say: “Knowledge is power”, but we actually believe data is actually the key. We consider data to be a strategic asset, as basis for the processes and decisions in the smart city, in which we are no longer users but actors in defining the urban life. Shall we dare call ourselves a “Wijze stad”, (literally translated wise city)? Why not?
